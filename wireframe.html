Today's Digest

Hawaiian Electric unveiled a $2.5 billion grid modernization plan this morning, the largest infrastructure investment in the utility's history. The plan targets 100% renewable energy by 2040 and includes new battery storage facilities on every island — a direct response to criticism following the Maui wildfires.

Meanwhile, Maui's tourism recovery hit a major milestone: visitor arrivals surged 23% year-over-year, exceeding pre-fire levels for the first time. West Maui hotels are reporting 85% occupancy, though residents remain divided on the pace of reopening.

In other news: Oahu's median home price climbed to $1.15M (up 8% YoY), Kauai hit a 95% renewable energy milestone after running on solar and hydro for 18 straight days, and a Big Island seaweed startup just raised $15M to triple production capacity.
